# heap-implement

An exercise to practice implementing heaps and OOP.

## Wave 0: Set up and plan
THIS WAVE SHOULD BE DONE WITHOUT AI

One partner forks and adds the other as a collaborator. Both clone.

Take a moment to look at `Heap.java`. It's pretty empty! Notice what you'll need to implement. Next, look at `HeapRunner.java`. Notice the TODOs you will need to fill in.

Make a plan with your partner of what the names / types of the methods you'll be implementing will be. Also consider what private variables you will need. v=You get to make design decsions here, there is not a perfect answer!

## Wave 1: Implement and test
THIS WAVE SHOULD BE DONE WITHOUT AI

Implement your heap. You must create a complete binary min-heap with an ArrayList as the backing array. Test it with unit tests as you go. Do not wait until you have all the functionality before writing your tests! Make sure to be making commits along the way. Also fill in the missing spots in the `HeapRunner.java`. Make sure to properly handle peeking/popping an empty heap! It will be up to you to decide what "properly" means.

## Wave 2: AI Review

In this wave you will be working with an AI to review your design and improve your code. On Canvas you will be turning in your conversation with the AI along with your PR.

BEFORE MAKING ANY CHANGES, MAKE A BRANCH CALLED ai_review

Then, copy your Heap implementation to an AI along with this message:

```
I am a Java student learning about heaps and wanting to practice good OOP. WITHOUT JUST REWRITING THE CODE FOR ME, please let me know what parts of this are and are not idiomatic, and help me spot any bugs:
```

Take its feedback seriously but skeptically. Don't feel like you have to agree with everything it says! Use it to help you guide how to improve your solution. Implement some of its feedback, paste back the new version of the code and see what it thinks. Make commits as you go! Iterate on this for a while until you've reached a clean solution.

You may consider starting multiple conversations with multiple AIs. Make sure you keep a log of all the conversations, you will be turning them in.

## Wave 3: Submission

Make TWO pull requests:
- one from the main branch
- one from the ai_review branch

Submit both PRs and all your conversations with the AI on Canvas.
